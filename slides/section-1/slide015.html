Key Takeaways

The essential ideas to carry forward

ML = learning from data, not explicit programming

Great for complex problems, fluctuating environments, and discovering hidden patterns (data mining).

ML systems are classified along three independent axes

Supervision level · Batch vs. Online · Instance-based vs. Model-based. These combine freely.

Data quality often matters more than algorithm choice

Ensure data is sufficient, representative, clean, and contains relevant features. Garbage in = garbage out.

Balance model complexity to avoid over/underfitting

Overfitting: model too complex, memorises noise. Underfitting: model too simple, misses patterns. Regularisation helps.

Always evaluate on held-out data you've never touched

Use a train/validation/test split. Tune hyperparameters on validation; measure final performance once on test set.

There is no free lunch. To find a well-generalising model, you have to try multiple models and use training & validation data to select and fine-tune. Then evaluate only once on the test set.
